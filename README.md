3. Subscription Status
Pie chart shows 73% of customers are not subscribed, while only 27% are.

Highlights a potential growth area for subscription-based offerings.

4. Purchase Behavior by Frequency
Boxplot shows Weekly and Monthly shoppers have higher variability in spending.

Annual and Quarterly shoppers show more consistent purchase amounts.

Useful for segmenting customers by engagement level.

5. Payment Method Preferences
Bar chart shows PayPal, Credit Card, and Cash are the most used methods.

Bank Transfer is the least preferred.

Line plot reveals Credit Card leads in total purchase amount, followed by PayPal and Cash.

6. Seasonal Distribution
Bar chart shows equal representation across Spring, Summer, Fall, and Winter.

Indicates balanced seasonal data, ideal for trend analysis.

7. Category Spending Patterns
Line and scatter plots show Clothing has the highest total purchase amount.

Followed by Accessories, Footwear, and Outerwear.

These insights help prioritize inventory and marketing efforts.

8. Top-Spending Categories (Average Purchase)
Clothing, Accessories, and Outerwear top the list for highest average purchase amounts.

Suggests these categories may contain premium or high-value items.
